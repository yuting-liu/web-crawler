    I walked in and was greeted with a smile and excited hello. Juan, the owner/cashier told me about the week's special that was going on. We ordered the Media Noche with avocados, the chicken empanada, the pico de mango pulled beef jibarito, hibiscus lemonade, cafe con leche, and churros, shared among three people.

    The drinks were perfect. I enjoyed the lemonade more and my friends enjoyed the coffee more. The coffee was very creamy and smooth, not something that they've ever had before. Additionally, the lemonade is aesthetically pleasing!!! Next, the Media Noche was a nice blend of flavors. The plantains made it extra special. The chicken empanadas was perfect. The outside was nice and crispy and the inside was super tasty! The best part was definitely the beef jibarito. One bite will bring an explosion of flavor into your mouth, a mix of sweet, tangy, and salty. The spices and sauce had a nice kick to it. Last came the churros. The outside was just the right crispy with a nice, warm soft inside. The greatest part of the churros is the homemade guava sauce that it comes with. Not gonna lie, I wanted to lick the plate after I finished.

    I really enjoy Nini's because the ambience was really casual but cozy, the food is unique, and the customer service is the best I've received. Without a doubt, this will be a place I want to be a regular at, and that I will recommend to everyone!

    This was the first restaurant I tried while visiting Chicago. The owner/chef was all smiles with arms wide open. He gave me a verbose tour of his menu, including free samples of his hibicius tea. That tea was actually pretty good. It could be a little sweeter for my taste, but its definitely refreshing.

    I started off with the empanadas. They were definitely a highlight of the meal. They were crusty on the edges, with the right amount of flakiness. The cheese filling was high quality and oozed with deliciousness.

    I got the Cubanita sandwich, cause it fit my mood. The bread was soft with a god chew. The ingredients were an uncommon combination to me, but seemed to work well. My biggest complaint though was it wasnt bursting with flavor. It had flavor, but it was much more subtle. It didnt come with any condiments, but their house guava sauce truly helped the moisture level of the sandwich.

    For desserts, I got the besitos. They had a nice, sweet taste. The texture was a tad flakier than I would like structure wise. Make sure you dont eat this alone, because you will feel gross and fat after eating the whole batch. Its seductively sweet, but will leave you with an upset stomach.

    I can tell the chef here is bursting with pride and excitement. He actually cares what people think of his food and goes above and beyond to offer a wonderful experience. Its definitely a unique, affordable restaurant among all the beer gardens I encountered while in Chicago.

    Wow!!! Just got back from Nini's! Crazy delicious!!! Juan is the man!!! Super charismatic guy!!! So helpful at explaining how their food is prepared. Very small menu, but everything we got was awesome!

    Each week they have a different special, and this week was a Slow-Cooked Pulled Lamb Slider with tzatziki, cheese and jalapeños! SO Tasty!!! The 'buns' of the slider are made out of smashed plantains which had the perfect crunch to moist ratio. The bun is basically like a latke/potato pancake. Juan and family: Please permanently add this slider to your menu!!!

    Out of the 3 sandwich options, we ordered the Media Noche. I loved that the menu was small and simple to the point that Nini's has perfected every dish. Add an egg to the sandwich and it's served omelette style with melted cheese oozing out of the middle.

    Hibiscus Lemonade hit the spot! Refreshing and delish! The Cafe con Leche I had with dessert was also ridiculously good!!!

    The Pulled Chicken Empanada was good, but not worthwhile compared to the other items we got.

    For dessert, we had the Besitos de Cuba! Very large dessert portion that we only had half of it. The fried tortilla chips were great, but the guava jelly and powdered sugar made it a little too sweet for my liking. However, this dessert was featured on TV, so my opinion should not matter. I'll be getting the Churros next time!

    The potato chips that they sell are great! The brand is 'Dirty' but I've never heard of it before.

    After reading some of the other comments, I now realize why everyone not only LOVES Juan!!! ... But also LOVES the awesome and authentic Cuban food at Nini's!!!

    The menu has just three sandwich options, but why would you need more? The Media Noche, $8, is the best -- sliced turkey, cheese, pickle and sweet plantains. It's pretty great. I also tried the chicken empanada, $3, which was tasty and nice with the salsa verde. I pre-ordered a churro, $2, and they made it fresh when I was finished with the rest, so it would be hot.

    The hibiscus lemonade, $3, was awesome! "It's mostly lemonade," the owner's son said, topped with the hibiscus tea brewed there.

    The owner's son greeted us with a friendly smile and welcoming attitude. His enthusiasm made me want to know more about the place, which has a fantastic reputation. The family is Cuban and Mexican, which shows in the culinary style.

    The dining room has several tables, booths and a counter along the front window. If needed, the staff will show you to the restroom, which is through a door and in the pizza restaurant next door.

    I'll be back to try the other sandwiches.
